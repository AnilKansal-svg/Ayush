Pulses (Dal)

Dals are unpolished and do not undergo any artificial polishing with water , oil, or leather. so that their maximum goodness, protien content reaches you.

The five step process ensures that grains are uniform and of premium quality. Pules are inherently gluten-free

Organic Pulses (Dal)

Organic dal is grown without the use of synthetic pesticides, chemical fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms (GMOs), making it a healthier and environmentally friendly choice. It is cultivated using natural farming methods that support soil health and biodiversity

Spices

Spices are natural flavorings made from seeds, roots, bark, fruits, or flowers of various plants. They are used to enhance the taste, aroma, and color of food, and many also have medicinal properties.

Common spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cloves are staples in many cuisines around the world. Besides adding flavor, spices can aid digestion, boost immunity, and provide antioxidants.

Nutrimixes

Nutrimixes are specially prepared blends of nutritious ingredients like grains, pulses, nuts, seeds, and sometimes dried fruits or natural sweeteners.

They are designed to provide essential vitamins, minerals, protein, and fiber in a convenient form. Nutrimixes can be used to make porridge, drinks, or snacks and are especially popular for children, elderly people, or anyone needing a health boost.

Poha

Poha is a light and nutritious Indian dish made from flattened rice.

It is quick to prepare and often cooked with ingredients like onions, mustard seeds, green chilies, turmeric, and fresh coriander. Some versions also include peanuts, vegetables, or lemon juice for added flavor and nutrition.

Besan

Besan, also known as gram flour or chickpea flour, is a fine, pale yellow flour made from ground chana dal (split Bengal gram).

Dry Fruits

Dry fruits are nutrient-rich fruits that have been dried naturally or through dehydration methods to remove their water content. Common types include almonds, cashews, walnuts, pistachios, raisins, dates, and figs.

They are packed with essential vitamins, minerals, healthy fats, and antioxidants, making them a popular choice for healthy snacking.

Hing

Hing, also known as asafoetida, is a pungent spice derived from the resin of the Ferula plant. Commonly used in Indian cuisine, especially in lentil dishes and curries, hing adds a distinctive umami flavor and aroma when cooked in hot oil or ghee.

Despite its strong raw smell, it mellows out in cooking and enhances the taste of food. Hing is also valued in traditional medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Millets

Millets are small, nutrient-rich grains that have been a staple in traditional diets for centuries.

They include varieties like jowar (sorghum), bajra (pearl millet), ragi (finger millet), and foxtail millet. Millets are naturally gluten-free, high in fiber, and packed with essential nutrients like iron, calcium, and B vitamins.
